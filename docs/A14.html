Appendix N A contradiction when computing the dissipation

The dissipation for an newtonian incompressible fluid is

ℰ=-1V⁢∫Vη2⁢(∂⁡vi∂⁡rj+∂⁡vj∂⁡ri)2⁢𝑑V (N.1)

If we assume a flowfield with ∂⁡vx∂⁡rx≠0 (which causes ∂⁡vy∂⁡ry+∂⁡vz∂⁡rz=-∂⁡vx∂⁡rx, because the divergence of the velocity field must be zero) and vanishing diagonal components of the jacobian ∂⁡vy∂⁡rx=∂⁡vx∂⁡ry=∂⁡vz∂⁡ry=∂⁡vy∂⁡rz=∂⁡vx∂⁡rz=∂⁡vz∂⁡rx=0 , we compute via the formula above a dissipation of

ℰ=-2⁢ηV⁢∫V(∂⁡vx∂⁡rx)2+(∂⁡vy∂⁡ry)2+(∂⁡vz∂⁡rz)2⁢d⁢V. (N.2)

The absolut value of the dissipation must be greater than zero, because all the terms in the integral are quadratic and our assumption was ∂⁡vx∂⁡rx,∂⁡vy∂⁡ry,∂⁡vz∂⁡rz≠0.

But if we compute the dissipation according to equation (5.19) like

ℰ=-ηV⁢∫Vω2⁢𝑑V (N.3)

we get for our flow field ℰ=0, because the vorticity of our velocity field is zero! This seems to be an obvious contradiction, and either raises some doubts about the validity of the derivation of equation (5.19) for example in Frisch (1995) or implies that our assumed flow field is unphysical.
